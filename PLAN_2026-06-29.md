# Plan — 2026-06-28

A snapshot of where we are, what our goal is, and what to do next. Written so
we can come back to it.

## Our goal (the whole arc)

Build the **Alberta-Plan base agent** on the physical Pong rig — four
components, all continual, all experience-only (no environment internals
cross the experience boundary; the host holds sensors/actuators, the container
holds the brain, they talk over sockets).

| Alberta-Plan base-agent component | What it does | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Perception** (obs → state `s`) | turns the warped grayscale frame into a recurrently-updated state | ✅ scaffold done |
| **Transition model** `M(s,a)→(s',r')` | imagine outcomes; used by Dyna/planning | ⚠️ one-step ✅, multi-step ❌ |
| **Value functions** (Horde of GVFs) | evaluate states; one predictor per (cumulant, γ) | ✅ working (H1/H2/H3) |
| **Reactive policies** `π(s)→a` (actor-critic) | emit the action to the servo | ⬜ not started |

The **far goal (Step 12, Intelligence Amplification)**: the agent's GVF
predictions become *human-facing signals* — "the ball will beat you on the
left in 0.3 s" — a computational exo-cerebellum for a human partner. The
servo joystick is the IA channel. Every GVF should be useful to someone, not
just an internal variable.

Roadmap from here (ALBERTA_PLAN §5): unify transition model (Step 8b,
keystone) → Horde (Step 3, done) → background Dyna + prioritized sweeping
(Steps 7, 9) → average-reward (Step 5) → options + option keyboard
(Steps 10, 11) → control / actor-critic (Step 4) → feature-finding (Step 2)
→ IA demo (Step 12).

### The one gate everything is stuck behind

**Perception loses the ball's identity through paddle contact.** When the
ball's blob merges with a paddle, bottom-up connected-components turns them
into one blob → the tracker loses the ball → a wrong ghost takes its slot →
the world model's state has no ball at the bounce → no amount of training
teaches a bounce. This was measured directly (the "more frames" experiment,
path 1 dead): at a clean approach frame there is *no track at the ball's
position*; a ghost coasts off-screen.

Secondary, separate issue: **W2 degrades with more frames** (0.857→0.781→0.725
over 1200/3000/5000) — long-run drift in the world model's net (plain Adam,
no weight decay; the `forgetting=0.999` ctor arg is RLS-era leftover that only
touches the Horde, not the world model).

## Generality (verified against the code, not just the plan)

The architecture is general by construction; Pong is the *test world*, not
baked into the code.

- **Universal scaffold (no Pong knowledge)**: z-score salience, behavioral-
  utility junk rejection, prediction-conditioned proposal ("each pixel →
  nearest predicted track within range; the rest is new"), DPC transition
  model, event discovery from the surprise histogram (median+12·MAD, finds any
  episode discontinuity), dynamic-rollout stop on the model's own surprise,
  controlled discovery by action-correlation, slot assignment by track-id
  liveness, TD(0) GVFs.
- **The only Pong-specific surface** is the *choice of cumulants* — and even
  those are stated in **discovered** terms, not hardcoded coordinates: "the
  ball" = the fast free-moving slot (discovered), "my side" = the controlled
  slot's x-line (discovered), "the controlled object" = the slot whose motion
  correlates with the action (discovered). To port to a new world: keep the
  scaffold, write new cumulant predicates in the same discovered-slot
  vocabulary.
- **The failure modes are general too**, not Pong bugs: identity-through-
  contact is "two objects occlude" (any occluding world); the rare-event wall
  is class-imbalance in online learning (fix = more frames / Emphatic TD, not
  architecture); W2 drift is a general online-representation issue.

## What to do next — priority order

### 1. (Immediate, blocking) Finish perception's identity-through-contact — proposal steps 2 & 3

**This is the gate.** W3b, Dyna rollouts, planning, and control are all
blocked on the world model getting clean ball data through a paddle bounce.
Step 1 (`propose_regions_conditioned`) is built; the next two steps are:

- **Step 2** — wire the conditioned proposal into the tracker: use it when
  tracks exist, fall back to legacy bottom-up when cold. This keeps the ball's
  track at its true (occluded, model-assisted) position through contact
  instead of losing it to a merged blob / wrong ghost.
- **Step 3** — measure W2/W3b. Success criterion (honest): at a clean
  approach frame there *is* a track at the ball's position, and the bounce
  signal in the training data is no longer wrong-sign.

Closes the listed departure *"perception → model is one-directional"* — the
loop becomes bidirectional (model hints carry velocity *and* identity through
contact). General (any occluding world), on-plan (Spelke "objects persist
through contact").

### 2. (Once #1 lands) Re-measure W3b and the virtuous cycle

With clean contact data, the world model's learned bounce correction should
stop being corrupted by old freeze-then-spike data. Either W3b flips green,
or we learn it's a *capacity* problem (sharp bounce in smooth Fourier/MLP
features) — at which point the on-plan fix is a **discrete-event prediction
target** (predict the bounce as an event and apply it) rather than a
continuous residual. Either way: honest multi-step imagination.

### 3. (Parallel, low-risk, housekeeping) Fix W2 long-run drift

Add weight decay or an LMS+Autostep-style forgetting on the world model's
*learned net* (not just the Horde readouts). Contained fix; protects the
representation thread (Step 1). Does not block #1, so run it in parallel.

### 4. (After W3b) Background Dyna + prioritized sweeping (Steps 7, 9) — the keystone's payoff

This is *why* the transition model exists. Once `M` can imagine a trustworthy
few steps, run **imagined transitions** to update the Horde faster than real
time, ranked by prediction error (prioritized sweeping). The Alberta Plan's
"scale with computation" bet — the agent gets smarter per frame by *thinking*.
The dynamic rollout we already built is exactly the right primitive for this
(imagine until unrealistic).

### 5. (The real frontier) Control — actor-critic over a safe prior (Step 4)

The one component with **zero code so far**. The plan (ALBERTA_PLAN §8e): a
`Q` planner as a *trust-gated residual learner over safe-guess physics* — a
safe prior policy (the existing safe-guess) plus a learned residual that only
fires when trusted. Off-policy, continuing, robust. This is what emits the
`action` to the servo. Needs the Horde (done) and ideally a working `M` (#2).
This is the step that turns the agent from an observer into a player.

## Update 2026-06-29 (evening): MD Decoupling shipped; Way B attempted + reverted

### MD Decoupling — SHIPPED (commit 72a97df), a measured win
Replaced plain Adam on the world model's learned net with Magnitude-Direction
Decoupling (Hagele et al. 2026, arXiv:2606.25971). The W2 long-run drift is
GONE: foresight now IMPROVES with training (0.786 -> 0.906 -> 0.882 over
1200/3000/5000 frames) instead of degrading (0.857 -> 0.725). The
magnitude-creep that silently shrank the directional update is fixed
structurally (direction pinned to a Frobenius sphere + learned per-row/col
gains); no weight decay. W1 still passes.

### Way B (discrete-event bounce target) — ATTEMPTED, MEASURED, REVERTED
Built it generally (per-slot event DETECTOR + learned EFFECT head, trained
only on event frames; effect learned from the stream, no hardcoded
vx->-vx). Then measured why it could NOT learn, and reverted (it would add
complexity without solving the problem).

THE MEASURED BLOCKER (the real finding): the bounce signal IN THE STATE is
smeared, laggy, and often missing. At a real GT bounce (ball vx flips at
frame t), the state's ball-slot vx:
  - LAGS by 1-2 frames (flips at t+1 or t+2, not t),
  - is SMEARED (magnitude ~0.005-0.03 over 2-3 frames, not the clean 0.014),
  - is MISSING on many bounces (the ball not in the state at the contact).
This is perception's EMA + no-snap re-acquisition + coast spreading the
velocity flip over several frames. So Way B's effect target (the residual at
the event) is NOT a clean spike -- it's the same smeared weak signal the
smooth head already learns. Way B does not escape this because the DATA is
the problem, not the architecture. The event LABEL is also unreliable: the
vel-residual at bounce frames is ~0 (the flip hasn't shown up yet), so a
vel-discontinuity detector doesn't fire on real bounces, and per-slot
thresholds are contaminated by slot recycling.

CONFIRMED: W3b is NOT a world-model-architecture problem (MD fixed the
dynamics/optimizer side; Way B's event architecture is sound but premature).
W3b is a PERCEPTION VELOCITY-QUALITY-THROUGH-CONTACT problem -- exactly what
WORLD_MODEL_PLAN.md 'W3 PAUSED' diagnosed, now re-confirmed by direct
measurement after the perception identity fix. The identity fix (commit
2cf0a70) got the ball INTO the state at contacts (0.182 -> 0.737); the
VELOCITY through the contact is still smeared/laggy.

### The real next unblocker (confirmed by measurement)
Perception velocity quality through contact, WITHOUT regressing W2. The
tension: a sharp velocity estimator (snap at re-acquisition) gives a clean
flip but caused the unphysical spike (the no-snap fix exists because of
this); a smooth estimator (EMA) avoids the spike but smears the flip over
~3 frames. The plan's named path: a position-derived velocity estimator
that doesn't regress W2, OR carrying a clean velocity through the coast.
This is the frontier. Way B's event architecture RETURNS (and will work)
once this lands -- the detector/effect split is still the right answer for
the rare-event wall; it just needs clean data to train on.

### BREAKTHROUGH: imagination works across a bounce (commit 9e714a8)
Two real changes; the combination makes multi-frame imagination work across a
bounce for the FIRST TIME.

1. FIXED a pre-existing physics-default LAYOUT BUG. The state is INTERLEAVED
   (slot s = state[s*4 : +4] = cx,cy,vx,vy) but _physics_default (and
   _is_unrealistic) read it as BLOCK (all positions, then all velocities).
   So the "keep moving straight" kinematics was garbled -- the imagined ball
   never advanced correctly (this is why imagination drifted and the ball
   crawled backward). The residual/reward heads still trained (they learn
   whatever mapping the state carries) so W1/W2 passed, but the rollout's
   kinematics were wrong. Present since the DPC rebuild. Fixed: read
   interleaved. W1 passes; W2 0.714 (relearning after the residual change).

2. WAY C (ContactNets principle, arXiv:2009.11193, adapted online): the
   bounce is COMPUTED, not learned. Discover surfaces by POSITION CLUSTER

   reflect moving slots across the plane in the rollout (v -> -e*v). Nothing
   discontinuous is learned -> the rare-event wall (that blocked Way B, the
   joint fix, the labeler) is sidestepped. The reflection fires in the
   rollout only -> cannot break W2 (the Way B coupling regression is gone).
