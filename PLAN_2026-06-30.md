## The policy vs the reflex (a clarification)

We did NOT choose the policy INSTEAD of the reflex. The reflex IS part of the
policy:
  - The **reflex** (safe prior) = "move my paddle toward where the ball will
    arrive." Simple physics, no learning. The always-safe FLOOR.
  - The **planner** sits ON TOP of the reflex: it uses the world model (the
    imagination) to imagine each action and pick the best future. It only
    takes over if it proves itself better than the reflex (the trust gate).

The planner USES the world model; it is not the world model. The world model
is the imagination (predicts the future, learns from the stream, passive).
The planner is the decider (picks the action, active, uses the world model).
The reflex is the fallback decider (no imagination, just tracks the ball).

Honest current state of the policy: it is BUILT and RUNS, but does NOT beat
the reflex (both let 7 opponent points through in sim). The trust gate
correctly keeps it on the bench. Beating the reflex is the open frontier.

## Update 2026-06-30 (afternoon): the policy's real root FOUND + three fixes shipped (`0e3dd12`)

Picked up the policy frontier ("make it beat the reflex"). Diagnosed it
frame-by-frame and found the planner NEVER DIVERGED (`differs=0`) — in its
head every action looked equally safe. Traced it through a chain of real
causes and fixed three of them (measured, suite green — 14p/1xf):

1. **Finite-surface reflection** (`_apply_contact_reflection`). The Way C
   bounce treated my paddle as an INFINITE WALL — the ball always bounced at
   the plane whatever my paddle's y, so every imagined action scored
   identically (safety score `min(ball_x)` was always the plane x). Fix: a
   surface reflects a moving object only if they OVERLAP along the tangent,
   else the ball flies PAST. Applied ONLY to the CONTROLLED surface (the
   my-paddle: its imagined y is reliable — the action moves it — and its
   finiteness is the planner's lever). Other surfaces keep the infinite-wall
   rule (their discovered `s_slot` can be STALE under slot churn, so reading
   their y lost real bounces — regressed W3b; caught and fixed by restricting
   the gate). Measured: the ball now flies past my paddle in imagination
   (safety score goes negative) instead of always 0.116.
2. **Full-horizon action commit (MPC)** (`Policy.COMMIT_WINDOW = HORIZON`).
   Commit=4-then-reflex made all rollouts converge to the SAME reflex
   trajectory — the 4-step twitch was too small to matter. Fix: imagine
   HOLDING the candidate for the whole horizon, pick the safest, execute one
   step, re-plan. Measured: discriminating frames 0 → 10/90.
3. **Position-delta action gain** (`_controlled_action_model`). The
   discovered paddle speed was ~5× too small (0.0036 vs true 0.0188) because
   it regressed the EMA-REPORTED velocity (which lags). Fix: regress the
   per-frame POSITION DELTA (the true displacement). Closer to true.

THE DEEPEST ROOT (diagnosed, partially fixed — the real frontier):

**Controlled discovery was BROKEN.** It picked the BALL or None, not the
paddle — so the "reflex" controlled the wrong object, and the measured
`prior = stay` was actually do-nothing vs do-nothing (both leaked 5-8).
PROVEN: force the controlled object to the my-paddle track (by position) and
the reflex leaks **0 points** (intercepts every ball). So the broken
discovery was the entire gap between "agent does nothing" and "agent plays."

WHY IT BROKE (the same perception-through-contact family as H1):
  - The paddle's SLOT churns: its track DIES at every ball-contact (the
    kind-gate makes the paddle REJECT the merged blob → it coasts → dies
    after MAX_MISS → restarts with a new id → a new slot). 11 ids / 11
    changes over 500 frames. So the old per-SLOT action correlation never
    accumulated a clean paddle signal and the fast always-present BALL won.
  - Rewrote `controlled_track` to correlate per TRACK-ID (churn-free) with
    object-permanence persistence (hold while live; on churn, the heir is
    the live track of the same SHAPE CLASS nearest the last position —
    bridges churn without action variation).

WHY THE REWRITE ISN'T ENOUGH YET:
  - The paddle's EMA-smoothed position LAGS the action by ~6 frames,
    smearing the per-frame delta, so the paddle's action-correlation is only
    ~0.33 (barely above the ball's ~0.29). The initial commit is a coin-flip
    and persistence can lock in the BALL.
  - Also: controlled discovery needs ACTION VARIATION, but a well-behaved
    reflex settles into mostly-STAY → the correlation signal vanishes → the
    commit can't be re-established after churn. The object-permanence heir
    bridge is the answer to this (re-recognize by position+kind, not action).

## Update 2026-06-30 (evening): THE AGENT PLAYS — controlled discovery fixed (`854399a`)

Implemented the "fix the paddle's laggy signal" next-step and it turned into
the win. **The reflex with DISCOVERED control now leaks 0 opponent points**
(was 5-8; matches the forced-paddle 0). The agent correctly finds its paddle
and intercepts every ball. This was the whole gap between "agent does

THE REAL BUG (pre-existing, found while debugging the lag) — the
action→signed-scalar map was `a < neutral → +1`, which for the sim's
`[up=0, down=1, stay=2]` encoding mapped BOTH up AND down to +1 — collapsing
the two opposite directions into one. This garbled:
  - the signed action↔motion correlation (paddle read ~0.33, barely above
    the ball — so the detector couldn't tell them apart),
  - the gain's axis/sign (picked x, wrong sign, too small),
  - the IMAGINED paddle's motion in `_apply_action_effect` (the imagined
    paddle moved the SAME direction for up and down → the planner could not
    discriminate actions — a SECOND root of the planner's "every action
    scores identically").
Fix: `_action_scalar` splits the non-neutral actions into two OPPOSING
halves (first +1, second −1), so `[up,down,stay] → [+1,−1,0]`. General for
paired-direction control; which physical direction is '+' is discovered from
the correlation's sign.

THREE STRUCTURAL FIXES on top of the scalar bugfix:
1. **Lag-aware signed correlation** (`_fit_action_motion`, shared by
   `controlled_track` + `_controlled_action_model`): a window-k delta
   `pos[t+k]−pos[t]` regressed on the cumulative action over the window
   absorbs the EMA lag (~6 frames) and recovers a clean signed corr (paddle
   0.89 vs ball 0.61) + a near-true gain.
2. **Explore-then-lock** controlled discovery: while EXPLORING (recent
   actions tried BOTH opposing directions — `_is_exploring`, general &
   sim-independent) we commit/correct on signed correlation (a clearly-better
   challenger corrects the wrong early ball commit); once the action becomes a
   committed one-directional strategy (the reflex) we LOCK to object
   permanence (hold while live; on churn re-recognize the heir by position +
   shape; no confounded correlation re-commit). The heir bridge runs in both
   phases so paddle track churn re-finds the paddle without action variation.
3. **Position-delta gain** with the correct scalar: gain now `(axis=1 y,
   −0.0121)` — correct axis & sign, ~65% of the true 0.0188.

PLANNER STATUS (separate frontier): the scalar fix let the planner finally
