## The policy vs the reflex (a clarification)

We did NOT choose the policy INSTEAD of the reflex. The reflex IS part of the
policy:
  - The **reflex** (safe prior) = "move my paddle toward where the ball will
    arrive." Simple physics, no learning. The always-safe FLOOR.
  - The **planner** sits ON TOP of the reflex: it uses the world model (the
    imagination) to imagine each action and pick the best future. It only
    takes over if it proves itself better than the reflex (the trust gate).

The planner USES the world model; it is not the world model. The world model
is the imagination (predicts the future, learns from the stream, passive).
The planner is the decider (picks the action, active, uses the world model).
The reflex is the fallback decider (no imagination, just tracks the ball).

Honest current state of the policy: it is BUILT and RUNS, but does NOT beat
the reflex (both let 7 opponent points through in sim). The trust gate
correctly keeps it on the bench. Beating the reflex is the open frontier.

## What to do NEXT (the real progress)

The codebase is verified green. Two fronts now, in priority order:

### A. The policy — make it actually beat the reflex (the main frontier)

The policy is built but doesn't beat the reflex. Two untried, specific fixes
(both honest, both measurable):

1. **Let the imagined action commit for more than one step.** Right now the
   planner imagines action `a` for step 0, then the safe prior takes over the
   rest of the rollout — so the planner only sees the *immediate* action's
   effect, not a sustained strategy. Fix: commit the imagined action for a
   short window (e.g. 3-5 frames) before the prior takes over. Measure: does
   the planner now beat the reflex (fewer opponent points)?
2. **Sharpen the imagined reward.** The reward head was trained on one-step
   real transitions; over a 30-frame imagined rollout it may be off-
   distribution and noisy. Fix: re-train the reward head on imagined states
   too, OR use the imagined *state* (does the ball cross my line?) as the
   signal instead of the predicted reward scalar. Measure: does the imagined
   return now discriminate good from bad actions?

Try #1 first (cheaper, more likely culprit). Then #2.

### B. The H1 regression — the perception ghost-at-the-wall (the shared root)

H1's xfail is the same perception-identity-through-contact frontier the
whole session chased, now measurable as a *specific* symptom: the ball is
dropped from the state as it passes behind the my-paddle toward the wall,

This is UPSTREAM of the policy too — the policy's trust gate leans on the
Horde, and a wrong-sign `my_side` GVF is a wrong human-facing IA signal
(Step 12's whole point). Worth attacking once the policy experiment (#A1)
